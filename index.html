A letter I should have written sooner

Ummehani,

I am writing this without trying to decorate the mistake. I started preparing for my new shop, and I was excited. I told people around me. I told friends, relatives, and people who asked about what I was doing. In the middle of that excitement, I did not tell you. You found out after many others already knew, and that hurt you. I understand now that the hurt was not really about the shop. It was about the place you believed you had in my life, and how my carelessness made that place feel smaller.

You are not my biological sister, but I have always kept you in my heart like a sister. That makes this mistake heavier. A brother should not make his sister feel like she is outside the circle of his happiness. When something good happens, the people who matter most should not have to hear it from others. They should hear it from us, directly, with respect. I failed to give you that respect at the right time.

I also know this did not happen in an empty place. I have made mistakes like this before. Maybe not the exact same mistake, but the same kind of thoughtlessness, the same habit of realizing late that I should have been more careful with your feelings. That is why I am not going to say, "It was nothing," or "I just forgot," as if those words make it smaller. Forgetting can still hurt. Being careless can still make someone feel unimportant. My intention may not have been bad, but the result was still painful for you.

I am sorry that you had to carry that feeling. I am sorry that instead of feeling happy about my news, you were left wondering why your brother did not think of you first. I am sorry that my excitement became a reason for silence between us. I do not blame you for becoming quiet. I do not blame you for being upset. If someone I trusted made me feel forgotten, I would also need time. Your silence has made me think, and I should have thought this deeply before the silence happened.

I cannot honestly promise that I will never make another mistake. That would sound good, but it would not be honest. I am human, and I know I can be careless when my mind is full. What I can promise is that I will not treat this as a small thing. I will remember how this felt for you. I will slow down before I share good news with the world and ask myself who deserves to hear it from me first. I will try to become more attentive, more thoughtful, and more careful with the people who matter most.

I am not making this to pressure you to forgive me. I am not asking you to forget what happened. I am not trying to win an argument or prove that my apology is enough. I only want you to know that I finally understand why you were hurt. You wanted to feel remembered, included, and respected. You deserved that from me, especially because of the bond we have always had. I missed that moment, and I am truly sorry.

The happiest news in my life should have reached you first. Not because of formality, not because anyone told me to do it, but because that is what family does. If my new shop becomes something good, I do not want that happiness to stand on a memory where I made you feel left behind. I want to carry the lesson with me. I want to remember that love inside a family is not only big words. It is attention. It is timing. It is telling the right person before the whole world knows.

Take whatever time you need. Be upset if you still feel upset. Stay quiet if that is what feels right for you. I will respect that. From my side, I am sorry with a clean heart. I hope one day this mistake becomes something I learned from, not something that keeps distance between us. And even if it takes time, I want you to know this clearly: you matter to me, Ummehani. You should have been told first.
